# Goblin Mode — Risk Register

*The honest list of what could go wrong, and what to do about it. For a solo, no-deadline project the biggest risks are scope and motivation, not technology. Revisit this at every milestone review.*

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
|---|------|------------|--------|------------|-------|
| 1 | **Scope creep / feature bloat** — the day/night + roguelike + sim + multiplayer combo is ambitious for one person | High | High | Ruthlessly protect the vertical slice; keep a "cut list"; defer multiplayer and extra biomes until the core is proven | You |
| 2 | **Core loop isn't fun in playtests** — sneaking/raiding doesn't land | Medium | High | Prototype the raid first and gate full production on a fun vertical slice; be willing to redesign or stop | You |
| 3 | **Solo motivation / burnout** — no deadline can mean no momentum, or grinding yourself out | High | High | Weekly playable build + devlog for visible progress; small milestones; allow breaks; share builds for encouraging feedback | You |
| 4 | **Multiplayer netcode swallows the project** — classic solo-dev trap | Low | Medium | Now scoped out: single-player at launch; co-op raids planned as a post-1.0 fast-follow; competitive "rival warren" is cut. Don't build systems speculatively "network-aware"; pay one cheap seam only (raid logic in a self-contained scene driven by an injected controller, simulation out of `_process`). See [decisions log](00-decisions-log.md) | You |
| 5 | **Procedural generation feels repetitive or breaks** | Medium | High | Strong hand-authored chunks; automated solvability checks; biome variety; tune via playtests. Studied precedent: *The Swindle*, whose procgen heists drew complaints of feeling samey — a direct warning to invest in hand-authored variety, not just more seeds | You |
| 6 | **Art/audio pipeline bottleneck** — one person can't produce all assets fast enough | High | Medium | Spend nothing until M2 clears its go/no-go; then put budget into ART first (a paid pre-production package — mood board + master palette + key/capsule art) and self-produce in-style, keeping audio in-house. Enforce hard launch content caps (1 polished biome, ~4 rooms, ~8 traits, 1 guard archetype + 1 patrol variant, 1 goblin body with palette/accessory variation) so the asset load stays bounded. See [decisions log](00-decisions-log.md) | You |
| 7 | **Stealth AI feels unfair or exploitable** | Medium | Medium | Prototype guard sight/noise early; extensive balance playtesting; clear feedback so players understand detection | You |
| 8 | **Market crowding** — many roguelikes and management sims compete for attention | Medium | Medium | Lean hard into the distinct "goblin mode" hook and comedic tone; build a Steam wishlist/devlog audience early | You |
| 9 | **Monetization underperforms** — premium indie sales are unpredictable | Medium | Medium | Strong store page + trailer + demo; consider Early Access for runway and feedback; keep production costs low | You |
| 10 | **Engine choice regret** — switching engines mid-project is very costly | Low | High | Godot 4 + GDScript is locked unless a hard blocker appears in the prototype. Don't switch engines after the prototype without a hard reason — a late switch throws away months of work. See [decisions log](00-decisions-log.md) | You |
| 11 | **Save-system bugs corrupt progress** — especially damaging given persistent warren | Low | High | Robust serialization, autosave + backups, test save/load across updates so patches don't break saves | You |
| 12 | **Tone misfires** — "goblin mode" humor reads as crude or off-putting | Medium | High | The [Tone Charter](08-narrative-and-glossary.md) is the proactive mitigation: spine is "underdog farce" (goblins are the butt of the joke as often as the tall folk; comedy from incompetence and slapstick, never from victim suffering), with concrete ALWAYS/NEVER examples. Carry a named playtest question — "is the humour landing as charming, not cruel?" | You |
| 13 | **2D lighting is gameplay, but Godot lights are rendering-only** — Godot's `Light2D` draws pixels but can't be read by the AI, so what the player sees and what guards "see" can silently drift apart | Medium | High | Run two parallel, intentionally-correlated systems: compute gameplay "lit-ness" analytically on the CPU (each lamp a data object — position/radius/occlusion; a goblin's exposure = the sum of unobstructed light reaching it, via raycasts against occluders), then author the visual `Light2D` to match the data lamps. Never read the framebuffer for gameplay. Prototype in M1. See [decisions log](00-decisions-log.md) | You |
| 14 | **Runway / break-even** — a solo passion project can quietly outrun its budget or never recoup its costs | Medium | Medium | Keep cash outlay small (Steam Direct $100, optional contractor pass, paid tools) and know the maths: net per copy after Steam's 30% is ~$10.49 at $14.99, so break-even is low hundreds to low thousands of sales. Decide the financial goal honestly up front (recoup-costs passion project vs income-replacing) — it changes the EA call, scope, and contractor spend. Context: only ~300 of ~20,000 Steam games released in 2025 cleared $1M revenue. See [decisions log](00-decisions-log.md) | You |

## How to use this register

Re-score likelihood and impact at each milestone review. Anything that becomes **High/High** gets an explicit plan or a scope cut *that cycle*. With multiplayer now scoped out (see #4), the top three to watch from day one are **#1 scope creep, #3 burnout, and #2 core loop isn't fun** — those sink more solo projects than any technical problem.
